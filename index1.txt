<section class="about" id="about">
   <h1 class="heading"><span>Coffee history and trends</span></h1>
   <div class="row">
   
       <div class="image">
	   <img src="images/prvaslika.jpeg" alt="">
	   </div>
	   
	   <div class="content">
	   <p>The history of coffee goes back to the middle of the 15th century and is very turbulent. At the very beginning it was used in religious ceremonies in Yemen,
while in Ottoman Turkey during the 17th century it was not used for political reasons (although this is not really supported by evidence).</p>
<p>The first coffee shop in Vienna was opened by a Serb - Franjo Đura Kolčić, who seized large quantities of coffee after the Turkish siege of Vienna.
and started selling them to the ladies of Vienna. They accepted it as a great awakening drink.
And then, following their example, coffee was accepted by other countries of Europe. On its journey through the centuries, coffee has established itself on all continents.</p>

<section class="about" id="about">
   <h1 class="heading"><span>Preparation and enjoyment</span></h1>
   <div class="row">
   
       <div class="image">
	   <img src="images/drugaslika.jpeg" alt="">
	   </div>
	   
	   <div class="content">
	   <p>Coffee is prepared in different ways and the way varies from country to country.
The most famous are Turkish coffee (or now better known as domestic or black in the Balkans), filter coffee, espresso, instant and Irish coffee.</p>
There are many alternatives. Let's say Turkish coffee can be boiled or roasted, filter coffee can be with more or less water,
espresso can be upgraded, so we get macchiato, extended espresso, coffee latte, cappuccino, instant coffee can be of different flavors,
and Irish coffee prides itself on the alcohol added to it.
What is common in recent years for coffee is that each of these types can be without the basic ingredient, which is caffeine.</p>
