History of coffe and trends

"The history of coffee dates back to the mid-15th century and is highly tumultuous. Initially, it was used in religious rituals in Yemen, while during the 17th century in the Ottoman Empire, it was not allowed for political reasons (although this is not well supported by evidence).

The first coffee shop in Vienna was opened by a Serb named Franjo Đura Kolčić, who seized large quantities of coffee after the Turkish siege of Vienna and started selling it to Viennese ladies. They embraced it as an excellent invigorating beverage. Subsequently, other European countries followed suit and embraced coffee. Throughout its journey across centuries, coffee has become a staple on all continents.

Preparation and enjoyment

Coffee is prepared in various ways, and the method varies from country to country. The most well-known ones are Turkish coffee (or now more commonly known as domestic or black coffee in the Balkans), filter coffee, espresso, instant coffee, and Irish coffee.

There are many variations. For example, Turkish coffee can be boiled or roasted, filter coffee can be brewed with more or less water, espresso can be enhanced to create macchiato, lungo, caffe latte, cappuccino, instant coffee can have different flavors, and Irish coffee proudly includes alcohol as an ingredient.

What has become common in recent years for coffee is that each of these types can also be enjoyed without the main ingredient, which is caffeine.
